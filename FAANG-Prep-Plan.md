## Your daily loop (the engine)

Every problem follows this cycle. It's slower than watching solutions — that slowness *is* the learning.

1. **Warm-up retrieval (~10 min):** re-solve 1 problem that's "due" from your spaced-review queue, from a blank screen.
2. **Attempt new problem (~20–25 min):** timer on. Try before looking at anything. Narrate out loud.
3. **Unstick in steps, not leaps:** hint → bigger hint → approach → solution. Each step you *don't* need is one you've earned.
4. **Study the worked solution** (more heavily now, less over time).
5. **Retrieval:** close everything, re-implement from scratch.
6. **Self-explain (write 2–3 lines):** why it works, the complexity, where it'd break.
7. **Tag the pattern** + schedule re-solves (day 3, 7, 21).

**Weekly cadence:** 5 days new-problem work · 1 day mixed **interleaved** review (random patterns, no warning) · once patterns build up, 1 timed **think-aloud mock**.
